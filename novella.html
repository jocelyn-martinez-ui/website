Jocelyn Martinez Close Case Study

CONCEPT DESIGN

Novella Reader

Solving the "Discovery Paradox" in digital reading through mood-based Information Architecture and sensory-focused UI.

01 / The Mission

Curation Over Commerce: A Design Intervention

Digital reading platforms have largely stagnated, evolving into high-friction marketplaces that prioritize "The Transaction" over "The Reader." Current industry leaders are plagued by legacy UI clutter interfaces that are over-saturated with "Top 10" banners and aggressive marketing carousels that reflect commercial bias rather than personal connection. Novella was conceived as a design intervention: a minimalist, premium sanctuary that strips away visual noise to restore the intimacy of the reading experience.

The Strategic Goal: To replace the outdated "Storefront" model with a "Sanctuary" model leveraging a sleek, modern aesthetic that prioritizes the reader's emotional state through Affective Discovery and conceptual matching.

Eliminating Outdated Clutter

I moved away from the grid-heavy, thumbnail-dense layouts of the 2010s. By utilizing Generous White Space and a high-contrast typographic hierarchy, Novella reduces the Cognitive Load that typically leads to choice paralysis in competitors.

Atmospheric Personalization

Rather than pushing "New Releases," I proposed a discovery path based on Atmospheric Markers. Users navigate by the "vibe" they wish to inhabit such as Rainy Afternoon in London or High-Stakes Melancholymaking the search process as evocative as the books themselves.

Innovative Hypothesis

The "Literary Synapse" Concept

To address the fundamental friction of "Discovery vs. Connection," I developed a conceptual matching engine titled The Literary Synapse. This proposed system moves beyond basic metadata, aiming to "pair" readers with authors based on prose-cadence and emotional resonance.

Voice-First Matching

A proposed algorithm that prioritizes the "Voice" of the writing matching users who prefer minimalist prose with Hemingway-esque styles, rather than just matching by genre.

The 5-Minute "Trial Run"

A conceptual UX flow where users engage in a "Blind Trial." This allows a reader to experience a curated excerpt without seeing the cover or title, ensuring a pure emotional match with the text before purchase.

*Note: The "Literary Synapse" and "Trial Run" features are currently in the conceptual design phase, serving as a vision for the future of hyper-personalized digital libraries.
02 / Competitive Audit

Benchmarking the Giants

To understand why the digital reading experience feels broken, I conducted a heuristic evaluation of three industry leaders: Kindle, Goodreads, and Libby. I evaluated them based on Visual Noise (cognitive load), Discovery Friction (how hard it is to find a book), and Immersion (the reading environment itself).

Conclusion: Most apps suffer from "Utility Over Aesthetic." They function perfectly as storefronts or databases, but fail to provide a sanctuary for readers.

Kindle

The Storefront
Visual Noise: High
Discovery Friction: Medium
Immersion: Low
UX Breakdown

Strength: A frictionless purchasing funnel and the massive Amazon ecosystem (WhisperSync).

Weakness: The interface is highly transactional. The home screen utilizes dark patterns to push "Kindle Unlimited" subscriptions and sponsored books, leading to severe cognitive overload before the user even opens a book.

Goodreads

The Database
Visual Noise: High
Discovery Friction: High
Immersion: N/A (No Reader)
UX Breakdown

Strength: Unmatched community social proof and a comprehensive cataloging system.

Weakness: Suffers from a severely outdated Web 2.0 Information Architecture. Finding a book requires navigating through cluttered, text-heavy lists. Reviews are prioritized over the book's synopsis, influencing user bias immediately.

Libby

The Library
Visual Noise: Low
Discovery Friction: High
Immersion: Medium
UX Breakdown

Strength: A clean, accessible interface with a highly intuitive onboarding process (using a physical library card).

Weakness: Discovery is severely limited by local library inventory. The UX introduces "Availability Anxiety" users often find a book they want, only to be met with a 12-week waitlist, halting the user journey abruptly.

The Opportunity

The Gap in the Market

Readers are currently forced to use a fractured ecosystem: they use Goodreads to discover, Libby to borrow, and Kindle to read.

  • Novella merges discovery and reading into one seamless, aesthetic-driven environment.
  • Replaces anxiety-inducing storefront UI with calm, mood-based filtering.
  • Prioritizes typography and white space over algorithmic "Recommended for You" carousels.
03 / User Personas

Psychographics Over Demographics

Traditional reading apps filter users by genre preference ("Sci-Fi" vs. "Romance"). Novella filters by emotional intent. To build a truly modern discovery engine, I developed personas based on behavioral archetypes and psychological typologies rather than just age and occupation.

  • The "Vibe" Matrix: Replaced standard genres with an emotional slider (e.g., Cozy vs. Tense).

04 / Visual Identity

The Sensory Interface

Digital reading often feels sterile. For Novella, I aimed to bypass the "coldness" of standard apps by creating a Sensory Interface. Every color and typeface was selected to trigger a tactile association, mimicking the weight of paper, the scent of ink, and the texture of a leather binding.

Color Psychology: The "Archive" Palette

#4C2727 | Heritage Burgundy

This isn't just a red, it's the color of oxblood leather. Psychologically, it conveys authority, history, and intellectual safety. It serves as our anchor, grounding the digital experience in tradition.

#E8E6E3 | Warm Parchment

Pure white (#FFF) causes "Ocular Glare." By using a high-chroma warm grey, we reduce blue-light strain and evoke the tactile warmth of physical paper, signaling to the brain that it's time to slow down.

#4A4742 | Charcoal Ink

Used for primary text. Pure black on white is too harsh for long-form reading. This charcoal variant provides optimal contrast ratios (WCAG compliant) while appearing softer and more organic.

#8B827B | Muted Taupe

Reserved for subtext and metadata. It creates a visual hierarchy that allows the book titles to shine while keeping secondary information accessible but "quiet."

Typographic Pairing: Elegance meets Utility

Headings / Playfair Display

Aa

The Editorial Choice

Playfair Display was chosen for its high-contrast strokes and "Modern" serif classification. It evokes the prestige of 18th-century European printing. In the app, it acts as the Emotional Hook, making every book title feel like a headline in a high-end literary journal.

UI & Body / Inter

Aa

The Precision Choice

Inter serves as the functional backbone. While Playfair provides the soul, Inter provides the clarity. It is a variable font designed specifically for computer screens, ensuring that small metadata (like page numbers and mood tags) remains legible even on low-resolution displays.

06 / Layout Strategy

Information Architecture vs. Visual Immersion

When transitioning Novella to the web, I faced a "Canvas Dilemma." Desktop screens offer vast space that often leads designers to over-complicate. I explored two distinct directions: one focused on feature-parity and another on psychological decompression.

VARIANT A

The Information-Dense Grid

Organizational Logic
  • Scanning Pattern: Utilizes a standard **F-Pattern** for high scannability.
  • Functionality: Closely mirrors mobile app features "in-your-face" for immediate access.
  • The Goal: Designed for high-speed management of large personal libraries.
VARIANT B

The "Editorial" Spotlight

Organizational Logic
  • Visual Hierarchy: Implements a **Z-Pattern** to reduce choice paralysis.
  • Gestalt Principle: Uses Proximity and Scale to create intentional "Focus Zones."
  • The Goal: Prioritizes an emotional connection to a single book over the utility of a list.

Design Synthesis: Why B Won

Ultimately, I moved forward with Variant B. While Variant A was more "efficient" at showing volume, it failed the brand’s mission of providing a sanctuary for readers.

"The design objective wasn't to build a better spreadsheet of books, but to recreate the feeling of walking into a quiet, curated boutique library."

01. The Aesthetic-Usability Effect Users perceive aesthetically pleasing designs as more usable. Variant B’s editorial layout reduces "Interface Friction" by making the user feel calm. Variant A, while functional, felt "loud" and cluttered, which triggers micro-stress in users looking to unwind.

02. Cognitive Load & Hick's Law Variant A suffered from Choice Paralysis. By forcing all features and dozens of covers into the immediate view, the user has to work to decide where to look. Variant B uses negative space to "give the user permission" to focus on a singular recommendation.

03. Intentional Friction I intentionally chose a "User-Friendly" editorial approach over an "Efficiency" approach. For a reading app, slowing down the user journey is actually a feature, not a bug.

07 / Final Reflection

The Concept of Atmospheric UX

Novella was born from a singular hypothesis: Digital reading platforms have sacrificed the "soul" of the library for the efficiency of the warehouse. As a self-directed concept and mockup, this case study allowed for an unfiltered exploration of how high-end aesthetic design can actually improve functional user retention and emotional well-being.

The "Friction" Paradox

In standard UX circles, friction is the enemy. We are taught to reduce clicks at all costs. However, during the design of Novella, I discovered a Paradox of Friction. For an app centered on mindfulness and escapism, the "speed" of an Amazon-style interface is actually a deterrent. By intentionally slowing down the discovery process through editorial layouts (Variant B), the interface signals to the user’s parasympathetic nervous system that the "work" day is over and the "reading" experience has begun.

The Ethics of the "Vibe"

Unlike modern algorithms that trap users in "Filter Bubbles" (showing them only what they have already liked), the theoretical engine for Novella focuses on Mood-State Discovery. This is a more ethical approach to personalization it respects the user’s current emotional capacity rather than just their past purchasing data. Designing for "vibe" rather than "genre" encourages a diverse literary diet.

Technical Evolution & Future Scoping

If this concept were to move from Figma to production, the next phase of development would focus on the intersection of Accessibility and Neural-Aesthetics.

01
Variable Font Customization

Moving beyond static type to implement Variable Fonts that allow users to adjust not just size, but 'optical size' and 'grade' to account for dyslexia or varying levels of visual fatigue.

02
Biometric Synchronization

An experimental exploration into Dark Mode automation that syncs the UI's color temperature (#E8E6E3 to Midnight) with the user's local circadian rhythm for optimal melatonin protection.

03
API Interoperability

Developing a "Universal Library" API that allows Novella to act as an Aesthetic Skin for other services (Libby, Project Gutenberg), proving that the interface can exist independently of the content source.
