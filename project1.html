Overview

The city of Holland, Michigan, thrives on tourism, particularly during the annual Tulip Festival. However, the current reliance on physical maps for navigating historical sites and tours creates challenges for tourists seeking a more interactive and engaging experience.

To address this, the Holland Walking Tour App was conceived to modernize the tourism experience. Designed to provide rich historical content and seamless navigation, the app caters primarily to older audiences while remaining intuitive for younger users (20+). Its scalability also allows for future content expansion beyond historical tours, supporting long-term growth in tourism engagement.

As the sole UX/UI designer, I led the project from concept to execution, shaping the app’s aesthetic and functionality. The goal was to create an accessible, visually appealing, and user-friendly interface that enhances the experience for all tourists.

By digitizing the previously static, physical map, the Holland Walking Tour App provides a more interactive and accessible way for tourists to explore the city. Set to launch in the summer of 2025, it redefines how visitors experience Holland’s rich culture and history.

Solution

The Holland Walking Tour App modernizes the way tourists experience Holland, Michigan. By transforming a static, physical map into an interactive and user-friendly digital platform, the app enhances accessibility, engagement, and convenience for visitors of all ages.

At its core, the app offers a seamless walking tour experience with features tailored to tourists' needs, especially during the Tulip Festival and beyond. Each element was designed with simplicity, clarity, and scalability in mind, ensuring both ease of use and future growth opportunities.

Key Features

• Interactive Map: A central, visually engaging map that guides users to their selected destinations. Real-time location tracking helps users stay on course, with intuitive markers indicating the stops on their tour.

• Custom Tour Creation: Users can build their own tours by selecting sites of interest. The app calculates the optimal route and provides a timeline of stops for easy navigation.

• Turn-by-Turn Navigation: Clear and simple walking directions tailored to users' preferences, ensuring they stay on track. Includes real-time updates as they approach their next stop.

• In-Range Popups: When users arrive at a site, a "story" page automatically pops up, presenting historical and cultural details about the location, complete with images and additional context.

• Tour Overview: A summary page showing the user’s selected destinations, estimated tour duration, and a swipeable card carousel with key details about each stop, including distance and images.

• Admin Management System: A dedicated admin webpage allows easy updates to the app’s content, ensuring it remains relevant and accurate for future events and tours.

• Accessibility & Scalability: Designed to accommodate older audiences while remaining appealing to younger users. The app’s architecture allows for the easy addition of new tours, themes, or events as Holland’s tourism expands.

• Completion Feedback: At the end of the tour, users are shown a summary of their journey, highlights from the tour, and options to explore other tours or return to the home page.

User Research & Insights

We surveyed 52 participants aged 55–75 who live in or visit Holland, MI, to better understand how they interact with walking tours and what they expect from a mobile app experience.

What We Heard

  • “Too much text on screen.”
  • “I wish I could just press play and listen.”
  • “I couldn’t tell what building I was supposed to be looking at.”
  • “Sometimes it crashes or doesn’t load outdoors.”

How I Applied These Insights

  • Simple, uncluttered navigation
  • Audio narration included at each stop
  • Text blocks limited to 100–120 words max
  • High-contrast colors with 12pt readable fonts
  • Map includes an in range pop-up and clear location pins

Design Goals

  • Make navigation intuitive and beginner-friendly
  • Reduce cognitive load with short, digestible content
  • Ensure users feel confident about where to go next
