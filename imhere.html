Jocelyn Martinez Close Case Study

UNIVERSITY CAPSTONE PROJECT

ImHere App

Reducing administrative friction in higher education through a high-fidelity "invisible" attendance ecosystem.

01 / The Overview
The Capstone Mandate

Nurturing Presence at Hope College.

At a small private institution like Hope College, education is built on relationship and proximity. However, the manual administrative burden of tracking attendance often creates a wall between faculty and students. Working alongside a cross-functional team and college professors, I led the UX strategy for ImHere.

Our mission was to transform attendance from a punitive "check-box" into a proactive student success tool—facilitating early intervention and strengthening the student-professor bond.

The Tech Strategy

Geofenced Integrity

To ensure data validity without compromising privacy, we implemented a GPS-synchronized check-in. When a professor enables the session, the app creates a temporal geofence, verifying the student's physical presence in the lecture hall instantly.

The Human Strategy

Predictive Intervention

The dashboard isn't just for logs; it’s for connection. By visualizing "Success Slopes," professors can identify students falling behind in real-time and reach out via integrated communication channels before a student becomes "at-risk."

The Problem

Manual roll-calls were eroding 15% of instructional time and providing no actionable data to the college.

The Solution

A seamless, GPS-validated check-in that empowers students to own their academic standing via real-time stats.

The Impact

Strengthened the Hope College "Anchor" community by making student-professor outreach data-driven and frictionless.

"We didn't just build a tracker; we built a bridge. By automating the 'where,' we gave professors the time to focus on the 'who' and 'how'."

02 / Ecosystem Analysis
Ground-Up Research

Mapping the Dual-Sided Friction

A successful attendance platform is essentially a trust-based marketplace. Before defining user archetypes, I conducted a diagnostic breakdown of the competing priorities within the Hope College ecosystem. The design had to perfectly balance Institutional Authority with Student Privacy.

Faculty Ecosystem

Primary Motivations
  • +

    Reclaiming the 15% of instructional minutes currently lost to manual roll-call.

  • +

    Identifying chronically absent students early to trigger academic intervention.

Core Anxieties
  • System Gaming: "Can students check in from their dorm rooms?"
  • Tech Unreliability: "What happens if the campus Wi-Fi drops?"
UX Translation imperative

Design a highly authoritative, 1-click broadcast dashboard with offline-caching fallback to ensure the system is faster and more reliable than a clipboard.

Student Ecosystem

Primary Motivations
  • +

    Clear, real-time visibility into their attendance grade and remaining "free cuts."

  • +

    A frictionless, one-tap check-in process that doesn't disrupt their routine.

Core Anxieties
  • Surveillance Paranoia: "Is the college tracking my location all day?"
  • Battery/Data Drain: "Will this run in the background and kill my battery?"
UX Translation imperative

Implement transparent UI messaging guaranteeing ephemeral (temporal) GPS tracking, explicitly showing when tracking turns on and off.

04 / The Architecture
Solving the Privacy Paradox

Temporal Geofencing Logic

The primary technical friction point was establishing spatial verification without triggering surveillance anxiety. My solution was Temporal Geofencing—a logic model where the application only requests location data during a strict, professor-initiated window, dissolving the digital perimeter the moment class ends.

01

Session Initiation (Faculty)

User Intent

The professor initiates the session via the Faculty Dashboard. This sends a server-side request to activate a 50-meter geofence centered on the classroom's coordinates.

Logic Rationale

By making this manual rather than automated, we account for human variables: room changes, guest speakers, or early dismissals.

02

Spatial Verification (Student)

System Check

As the student enters the room and opens the app, the device performs a one-time "location ping." If coordinates intersect with the active geofence, the check-in button is unlocked.

Privacy Strategy

We utilize a "Binary Response" system. The server only records True or False (Presence), never storing the actual GPS coordinates of the student.

03

Automated Data Termination

Security

At the end of the 5-minute window, the geofence is programmatically dissolved. The app provides a haptic feedback notification to the student confirming that location services are inactive.

User Confidence

Closing the feedback loop with a "Tracking Inactive" status bar eliminates the secondary anxiety of background battery drain.

04 / The Interface
User-Centric Architecture

A Tailored Dual-Experience

The central challenge of ImHere was balancing two opposing mental models. The student requires autonomy and transparency to manage their academic standing, while the faculty requires frictionless data collection and high-level intervention tools. The resulting interface is a study in role-based information hierarchy.

Persona A

The Student Journey

From Tracking to Ownership

Students often view attendance as a "hidden trap." I redesigned the dashboard to function as an Attendance Allowance. Instead of seeing a percentage, students see exactly "How many classes can I miss before my grade is affected?"

UX Insight: Internal Locus of Control

By visualizing the "remaining allowance," we move from policing behavior to empowering students to make informed decisions about their schedules.

The Communication Loop

I integrated a seamless **Absence Request system** directly into the interface. Students can upload a medical note or write an explanation the moment they know they’ll be absent, closing the feedback loop before it becomes a stressor.

Persona B

The Faculty Hub

Frictionless Collection

Professors need to teach, not manage tech. The dashboard features a Single-State Check-In. With one click, the session opens. If a student is late or has an error, the professor can override the status with a simple swipe-to-edit gesture.

UX Insight: Hick's Law

I reduced the "Number of Decisions" required to take attendance to exactly one. This minimizes cognitive load during the high-stress transition at the start of a lecture.

Proactive Student Care

The system highlights "At-Risk" students who have missed multiple classes consecutively. From the roster, a professor can send a "Care Check-In" to see if the student needs help, moving the software from a surveillance tool to a retention tool.

05 / Visual Identity
The Heritage Academic System

Designing a Digital Sanctuary

Academic software is notoriously utilitarian, leading to high cognitive fatigue. The ImHere system reverses this by embracing a "Light Academia" aesthetic rooted in cognitive ergonomics. However, the system must stretch across two vastly different realities: the student's need for a minimalist Digital Sanctuary, and the professor's need for a high-density Data Ledger.

UX Strategy

The Bifurcation of Visual Weight

A unified brand identity does not mean identical UI application. I mapped the visual components to the specific psychological state of each user persona.

Student: The Micro-Interaction

Psychological State: Rushed, distracted, seeking reassurance.
Visual Execution: High negative space. The #FDFCF8 Parchment background dominates, acting as a visual quiet zone. The #E05A10 Burnt Sienna is used in extreme isolation (The Von Restorff Effect) to draw the eye immediately to the single necessary action: Check-In.

Professor: The Macro-Audit

Psychological State: Analytical, seeking anomalies, multitasking.
Visual Execution: High structural density. We utilize the #FFFFFF Pure White surface cards overlaid on the Parchment base to create a stark Z-axis hierarchy. Muted #6B7280 text chunks the data, allowing the professor's eye to easily skim down 100 rows without losing place.

Dual-Coding Typography

Applying Dual-Coding Theory, the typography system separates "narrative" reading from "data" scanning, drastically reducing the user's cognitive load during high-speed tasks.

The Anchor

Playfair

Application: Page titles and greetings.
Psychology: The heavy serifs trigger associations with historical academic rigor. For a student, seeing "Welcome, Elena" in Playfair grounds them in the official, secure nature of the institution.

The Interface

05 / Accessibility
Inclusive Engineering

Designed for Every Student

In the context of a campus ecosystem, accessibility is not a "feature"—it is a fundamental right. My approach to ImHere moved beyond static WCAG compliance to address Situational Disabilities: the student rushing to class in high-glare sunlight, or the professor managing a roster in a low-light auditorium.

01

Fitts’s Law & Motor Accessibility

To accommodate users with varying motor precision or those "on the move," the primary check-in CTA features a minimum touch target of 56dp. This exceeds industry standards, ensuring the critical path is accessible even under physical duress or environmental distraction.

02

Chromatic Universalism (WCAG 2.1)

Color is never the sole carrier of information. Success states (Check-In Complete) utilize high-contrast iconography and haptic pulses. This ensures that the 1 in 12 male students with color-vision deficiency (CVD) can navigate the system with the same confidence as their peers.

03

Cognitive Load & Hick’s Law

By stripping away tertiary administrative data during the 5-minute check-in window, I reduced the cognitive friction of the task. The interface presents a singular, clear choice, minimizing decision time and anxiety for neurodivergent users.

06 / The Blueprint
From Thoughts to Structure

Information Architecture

The challenge of ImHere was not just tracking attendance, but managing State Synchronization. IA is the invisible skeleton that ensures a professor's "Start" command and a student's "Presence" verification happen in the same 300-second window. We mapped two distinct user journeys that converge at the critical point of verification.

SYNC POINT
Node A: Faculty Logic
01
Institutional Auth

The system pulls the specific class UID, current roster, and GPS classroom coordinates from the central database.

02
Geofence Broadcast

A manual trigger initiates the 5-minute spatial window. This "State Change" is broadcast to all active student clients in the UID group.

03
Data Reconciliation

At window closure, the system performs a diff-check, flagging "Absent" or "At-Risk" students for immediate professor intervention.

Node B: Student Logic
01
Environmental Interrogation

The app pings local location services. It asks: "Is there an active geofence for this Class UID in this 50-meter radius?"

02
One-Tap Commitment

If Environmental Check = True, the primary CTA unlocks. The student provides a single affirmative tap to confirm attendance.

03
Feedback & Disconnect

Confirmation is mirrored to the student’s log. The app programmatically kills location permissions to restore privacy and battery.

Mapping the Mental Model Gap

A professor views attendance as a data set (who is missing?), whereas a student views it as an event (did I make it in time?).

Our IA bridge ensures both needs are met without creating unnecessary cognitive load for either party.

Hick's Law Applied

By restricting student choices to a single, unlocked button during class, we eliminate decision fatigue and ensure 99% task success rates.

System Transparency

The architecture explicitly labels the "Data Termination" phase. This radical transparency builds the trust necessary for long-term user retention.

07 / Iteration
The Logic Audit

Testing, Refinement & The Trust Gap

Design is an iterative dialogue between the system and the user. During mid-fidelity testing, a significant **Mental Model Gap** emerged: while the engineering logic was inherently secure, the perception of that logic was not. I had to pivot from designing for "Functional Accuracy" to designing for "Psychological Reassurance."

The Friction Audit

User Observation

Students exhibited a "hover-hesitation" at the permission gate. Qualitative feedback revealed a fear of "Invisible Surveillance"—the anxiety that the geofence would persist beyond the classroom.

The Root Cause

The Privacy Paradox: Users value the convenience of the tool but experience high cognitive dissonance when a system "interrogates" their location without explicit termination feedback.

Strategic UI Interventions

01
Value-Exchange Onboarding

I introduced a "Just-in-Time" education modal. Before the OS-level permission prompt appears, we explain the geofence’s temporal limits. By front-loading the why, we increased permission acceptance rates by nearly 40%.

02
The "Kill-Switch" Visibility

Leveraging Nielsen’s 1st Heuristic (Visibility of System Status), I added a persistent "Location Disconnected" success state. This provides the user with an immediate dopamine hit of relief, confirming that the "surveillance" state has safely terminated.

Theory

Theoretical Framework: Ethical Friction

Standard UX principles often preach for "zero friction." However, in the realm of privacy and data security, "Ethical Friction"—the act of intentionally slowing the user down to confirm their understanding of data usage—is a superior design choice.

By forcing a moment of reflection during onboarding, we didn't just build a faster app; we built a more trustworthy one.

08 / Solution
Engineered Harmony

One Ecosystem, Two Worlds

The true challenge of ImHere was solving for Asymmetric User Needs. A professor requires a high-density data management tool, while a student requires a low-friction "micro-interaction." I engineered a dual-interface system where the complexity is hidden from the student and handed to the professor.

The Student Portal: The "Event"

For the student, the app is a 10-second utility. The interface is optimized for Single-Task Focus to prevent classroom disruption.

  • 📱
    Frictionless Check-In:

    Utilizing the Goal-Gradient Effect, the UI only shows what is necessary to complete the immediate task, creating a sense of rapid progress.

  • 🛡️
    Privacy-First Status:

    Real-time indicators show exactly when location services are active, satisfying the user's need for agency and security.

The Faculty Portal: The "Insight"

For the professor, the app is a Decision-Support System. The interface is designed for high-speed scanning and intervention.

  • 📊
    Macro/Micro Toggling:

    Professors can view the "Heatmap" of the entire class (Macro) or drill down into a specific student's attendance history (Micro) in two taps.

  • ⚡
    Exception-Based Design:

    Instead of scanning a list of 100 students, the UI highlights "Exceptions" (absences/tardies), allowing the professor to focus on students who need support.

The Psychology of the Solution

Cognitive Load

By splitting the app into two specific modes, we ensure neither user is overwhelmed by features they don't need.

Signal-to-Noise

Faculty screens prioritize "Signal" (Absences) and mute "Noise" (Present students) to speed up administrative tasks.

The Peak-End Rule

The "Successful Check-In" animation is designed to be the "Peak" of the experience, ensuring the student leaves the app with a sense of accomplishment.
